The 3D Car project is a project in which we learned about the engineering design process to construct 3D car

The steps were as follows:

  • Learn how to Engineer draw and measure the car
  • Use Cad to input exact measurements of the car
  • Utilize the modeling features on Cad.OnShape and take away the negative space from the original block
  • Use Cura to transfer the car into a format to 3D print, use the brim setting
  • Finally, take a wooden block, saw it, sand it, and then paint it to create a platform for the car
  • Also decorate the car using strips from the vinyl cutter

The process:

Once I measured my car and knew what I wanted to construct (based on my selected toy car), I played around extensively with the settings, with the basis of trial and error to eventually get the form I wanted. The tool that I used the most was the extrude tool. Using both the “extrude add” and “extrude remove” I could not only get the basic form of my car but also add all the subtle design features of my car. For example, in the hood of my car, there is extensive use of the extrude add function in which I layered different parts at different levels to give the car more design elements and form.

For the block, I first cut the wooden block with a saw and this gave me very rigid corners and a rough surface. Next, to make the block more appealing, I spent a lot of time sanding the block, with different levels of sandpaper to make the block as smooth as I could and to round out the corners. Not only does this make the block feel better, but it also allows the paint to stick to the surface better, and appear more fluid. Additionally, using the laser cutter, I added a painted wooden name tag for the car. For the car design, I took segments from the vinyl cutter and either pasted them on or used scissors to manipulate them to put on my car. With this, the stickers added a lot more color and fun design to the car, especially with the face I made on the front.

Challenges, setbacks, and solutions:

My single biggest setback in the car design was the wheels. On OnShape, for some reason, there was an extrusion plane at the bottom of my car that prevented the wheels from being perfectly round. After attempting to use a lot of the features on OnShape, I eventually learned that I had to add a brand new part to the car as this would prevent the extrusions from being removed. This was not a difficult fix but it was helpful to learn more about the function of the application. However, when I transferred the car to the 3D printer, the wheels got cut off slightly again, but I do not mind the look of it. Simply, it looks like my car is “slammed” (a mechanic term when the car is put close to the ground for style.)
